You are absolutely on the right track with the **Terracotta (`#C25634`)** for the sun—it is the visual anchor of the brand. However, using **Deep Green** for the earth line presents a specific challenge in your Dark Mode ("Deep Forest") which we need to solve.

### 1. Are these the "Right" Colors?

**The Sun (Terracotta `#C25634`):**

* **Verdict: YES.** This color is perfect. It represents the "First Light" and "Idhanth" (Brightness). It has excellent visibility against both the Cream background (Light Mode) and the Deep Green background (Dark Mode).

**The Earth/Hill (Deep Green):**

* **Verdict: CONDITIONAL.**
* **In Light Mode:** Deep Green works beautifully. It represents "Ishya" (Spring/Growth) and contrasts well against the paper background.
* **In Dark Mode:** **NO.** If you place a Deep Green earth line on top of your "Deep Forest" background (which is *also* deep green/black), the logo will disappear.



### 2. The Solution: The "Chameleon" Logo

To maintain the Wabi-Sabi aesthetic and visibility, the logo must adapt its "Earth" color based on the theme. We will treat the "Earth" element as a variable.

#### **A. Light Mode (The "Paper" View)**

* **The Metaphor:** Spring dawn over a grassy hill.
* **Sun:** Terracotta (`#C25634`)
* **Earth/Hill:** **Forest Green** (or the Moss Green `#5D6B4D` from your palette).
* **Result:** High contrast, organic, grounded.

#### **B. Dark Mode (The "Deep Forest" View)**

* **The Metaphor:** The sun rising, illuminating the earth with a golden glow.
* **Sun:** Terracotta (`#C25634`)
* **Earth/Hill:** **Antique Gold** (`#D4AF37`).
* **Result:** The "Earth" line shifts from Green (pigment) to Gold (light). This aligns with the Kintsugi theme—the earth is illuminated or "repaired" with gold.

---

### 3. Visualizing the "Abstract Y / Horizon"

Here is how we execute the "Abstract Y" to fit your Horizon concept using these colors.

**The Shape:**
Imagine a lowercase **"y"** or a branching sprout.

* **The Right Branch (The Sun):** This is the larger, curved stroke (the Sunrise). It is **Terracotta**.
* **The Left/Bottom Stem (The Earth):** This is the grounding stroke.
* In **Light Mode**, it is **Green** (A stem growing from the earth).
* In **Dark Mode**, it is **Gold** (A reflection of light).



**Technical Execution (SVG):**
You do not need two different image files. You can use a single **Inline SVG** and use CSS classes to change the fill color of the "Earth" path.

```html
<svg viewBox="0 0 100 100" class="logo-mark">
  
  <path d="..." fill="#C25634" class="logo-sun" />
  
  <path d="..." class="logo-earth" />

</svg>

<style>
  /* Default (Light Mode) */
  .logo-earth { fill: #5D6B4D; } /* Moss Green */

  /* Dark Mode Override */
  [data-theme="midnight"] .logo-earth { fill: #D4AF37; } /* Gold */
</style>

```

### Summary Recommendation

1. **Keep Terracotta** for the Sun/Arch element in all modes.
2. **Use Green** for the Earth/Stem element in **Light Mode**.
3. **Swap Green for Gold** for the Earth/Stem element in **Dark Mode** to ensure it pops against the dark background.

This creates a beautiful interaction: when the user toggles the theme, the logo doesn't just invert—it **transforms** from organic growth (Green) to illuminated wisdom (Gold).
